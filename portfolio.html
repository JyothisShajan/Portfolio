Research Paper Computer Vision

Multi-Modal Edge Detection for Autonomous Driving

Fused RGB camera images with projected LiDAR depth maps for boundary-precise edge detection, using a dual-encoder ResNet-18 with channel-attention fusion at three spatial scales.

Designed a four-term loss (Boundary IoU, Dice, Depth Gradient, BCE anchor) and trained on 1,000 KITTI samples, achieving Boundary F-score 0.9874 and AP 0.3557 — outperforming a camera-only baseline.

NLP / LLMs 🧠 Emotions

Multi-Modal Emotion Detection

Built a multi-label emotion classifier (anger, fear, joy, sadness, surprise) over 2,143 English text samples, comparing zero-shot vs. few-shot prompting with Flan-T5-Base.

Few-shot prompting lifted weighted F1 from 0.475 to 0.501, with fear recall jumping from 0.187 to 0.323, and surfaced nuanced failure modes around implicit emotional language.

Python Hugging Face Flan-T5 Prompt Engineering Scikit-learn

Knowledge Graphs ⚽ Graphs

Knowledge Graph Link Prediction — Footballer Network

Constructed a football player knowledge graph (3,931 nodes, 18,194 edges) from Wikidata via SPARQL, benchmarking TransE (MRR 0.051) vs. RotatE (MRR 0.197) with PyKEEN.

Built a GraphSAGE GNN in PyTorch Geometric, achieving AUC-ROC 0.8249 and decisively outperforming traditional embedding-only models.

Deep Learning Generative

Variational Autoencoder — Fashion-MNIST

Implemented a convolutional VAE from first principles in PyTorch with strided encoder–decoder, BatchNorm, reparameterization trick, and ELBO loss (reconstruction + KL divergence).

Ran ablations over latent size (2D–64D), KL weight λ, and capacity, achieving reconstruction MSE 0.0145 vs. 0.1850 baseline with 15/15 automated sanity checks passing.

Story

About & Journey 🌍

I grew up in Mumbai, taking things apart long before I knew how to put them back together. That curiosity gradually shifted from circuits to code — and eventually to data: how people behave, how systems scale, and why some ideas compound while others stall.

My background blends electronics and computer science engineering with business analytics and product-thinking roles. At GRG India, I learned how much impact you can create by simply making complex data legible — building Python pipelines over tens of millions of records and SQL models that plugged directly into growth decisions.

Today, as an MSc Computer Science (Data Analytics) student at the University of Galway and a Graduate Teaching Assistant, I move between research and delivery. I like work that sits close to real-world constraints — whether that’s autonomous driving perception, knowledge graphs for messy domains, or analytics that give teams the confidence to ship.

Long-term, I want to keep building systems at the intersection of ML, experimentation, and sustainable growth — where models, metrics, and humans all stay in the loop.
